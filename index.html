BooksMap Mobile Application Mockups

In this project, I prototyped an application that enables students at the University of Michigan to find inexpensive used books, or sell unneeded books to others. This application ahs three unique features: using a map to locate nearby users and facilitate contact and know them; providing 2 options for users to post or search information via Have or Need ; paying with money or creative ways such as a dinner or a movie, etc.
See Paper Prototype.
Target Population
Students at the University of Michigan
Skill
Interaction Design.
Paper Prototyping.
Storyboarding.
Tool
Adobe XD.

Problem Statement

Students often find books through three methods: borrowing in libraries, buying in bookstores or purchasing online. However, books in libraries are often in short supply, those in bookstores might be expensive, and it takes time for shipping if purchased online. What if students want to get books instantly and at low cost? Ai the same time, there are some students who want to get rid of unneeded books. So how could those students connect with each other?

Solution Overview

I am providing students access to inexpensive books while providing others an outlet for their unneeded books. I designed an application which allows both groups to share the information of books they have/want, prices they feel comfortable with and a good location to exchange the books. This application allows them to reach the nearest users and connect with them.
This application works best when there are a lot of users, so I decided to firstly narrow down the community to just students on campus, where a large amount of students live. To avoid the problem of users not finding books and never coming back to use this APP again, I give users alternative option to post their needs first. Additionally, in order to attract more users and make this platform interesting, I will add one feature that users can pay with money or creative ways such as a dinner or a movie, etc.

Walkthrough

Users can sell or purchase books through two main methods: one is their interests and the other is searching on the map. During this process, they can sort the information by time, by price or by proximity in dropdown menu.

Users will be asked to selet their topics of interest when registering. This information is used to tailor their homepage. On the homepage, users can scroll down to see all interests or swipe to see all the books under this interest. Of course they can edit and check each.   (click on images)

Design Process

Initial Design.


The initial design of my application also had a function that allows users to rent and borrow books from libraries that have a bookshelf for used books and a librarian who is in charge. Information on books is also provides on the APP together with proximity. This application also provides book information from libraries and bookstores as alternatives to compare prices and quality at the same time. This idea was generated from one of my sketches (Figure 1).


Figure 1. Sketch in 8 solutions’ storyboard

Critical Features.


  • •  Directly connect users who need books with those who can provide idled second-hand books

  • •  Provide information retrieving feature of “Second-hand Books Library”

  • •  Provide information of the same books from school libraries and bookstores

Reflection.


According to the reading the Design of Everyday Things by Donald A.Norman, well-designed products should be clearly used with lessinstructions or even without any text-based information. Just thevisual, acoustic information themselves, being consistent with as many as possible of the four constraints, are enough to guide users to utilize them properly. I can’t agree more on Donald’s standpoint, so consider it as my most important design philosophy.
However, it is hard to achieve. After I built my paper prototypes(Figure 2 & 3) and showed them to my peers, they felt really confusedabout each feature, even though I explained all the time. Good design should be introductions the products themselves, but I explained a lot because they were way too much complex.


Figure 2. Too much content in one page and a lot of text near each button


Figure 3. Crowed layout makes users hard to understand

Changes.


It’s easy to get lost when designing the features of application, adding tedious details and redundant function buttons or pages, which I think would be necessary for users. But, that’s not true, because that’s the application design for designer myself, not users.
Only this one feature- share books with peers- already keeps this application complex, therefore, I decide to abandon the idea of “Second-hand Books Library” and keep the feature of “compare with other resources” as one simple button. I made this decision is not only because it follows my design philosophy, but also because a lot of target users and peers think it’s unnecessary.
After showing my paper prototypes to my peers and target users, I got a lot of feedbacks from them and added those buttons or indicators which I think proper right after they recommended. For example, I added slider on homepage (Figure 4) and I changed the drawer-shape menu to hamburger menu (Figure 5), which takes less space and wouldn’t cause confusion.


Figure 4. Homepage Sketch


Figure 5. “Want” page


Figure 6. “Have” page
One main change that I made, which was suggested by several users, was I added one page to show all the user who have/want this book when clicking the book image on homepage, instead of leading to individual book page which posted by one user each time. Because of this change, I simultaneously moved some buttons (Figure 4.location button and user profile button) from homepage to this page (Figure 5 & 6), making homepage much more clean and simple.
Another main change that I made was I added “Want or Have” (Figure 5 & 6) option for users. It is hard to let this application work if there are only few users, because only if there are enough users, there would be enough books. Imagine if you wanted to find one book on this App, but found there was no one posted any information about this, you would feel frustrated. But if you have tried several times, things are still the same, you would feel disappointed and never turn back. In order to avoid this happening, I provide users the option of choosing “Want” as your purpose, which means even if there is no one “Have” this book right now, users can post needs first and let others to search their needs.

File Management Consulting for Student Academic Affairs (SAA)

In this project, our team came up recommendations for Student Academic Affairs (SAA) in the University of Michigan College of Literature, Science and Arts (LSA) to alleviate the file management problem. We broke down the problem into 3 parts: technology (how to use Google Drive and M+ Box), naming protocol and implementation. My role was diving into the implementation section, finding out reasons why staff in SAA not following naming protocel and proposed recommendations.
See SAA website.
Client
UMichigan LSA Student Academic Affairs Office.
Skill
Contextual Inquiry.
User-Centered Design
Tool
Affinity Wall.

Problem Description

LSA Student Academic Affairs has multiple locations and mediums for storage of information (email, attachments, Google Drive, M+Box, internal websites, external websites), which makes it difficult to find information, as well as ensure information is consistent across all mediums. LSA Student Academic Affairs would like help in developing a process/guideline for how to store information. The current process is not being adhered to by the employees due to ease of use, lack of accountability, and lack of a logical process flow.


Methodological Overview:



Collect Data.


    •  Interviews
  •   ·  Ask Questions
  •   ·  Observing Workflows
    •  Background Research
  •   ·  M+Box
  •   ·  Google Drive
  •   ·  Naming Protocol
  •   ·  Rule Adherence

Analyze Data.


    •  Interpretation Session
  •   ·  Review Interviews
  •   ·  Take Annotated Notes

Synthesize Data.


    •  Affinity Wall
  •   ·  Affinity Notes
  •   ·  Walkthrough Sessions
    •  Feedback
    •  Brainstorm

Key Findings

Key Finding 1:

Staff feel protocols don’t reflect their needs and perspective.


  •   •  “A good idea I’ll never use”
  •   •  A perception that top-down protocols are ineffective or irrelevant
  •   •  Lack of voice in design process
  •   •  Staff use ad hoc file management strategies they find personally useful, and that are not driven by the 2014 protocol

Key Finding 2:

Users lack understanding and training


  •   •  Staff relied heavily on Google Drive for their everyday workflows, but most struggled to understand its features and behaviors.
  •   •  M+Box is a distant-feeling system that staff feel uncomfortable using
  •   •  Confusion about how to approach file management & file migration from old systems

Key Finding 3:

Organizational change resistance


  •   •  Continued “shadow use” of Shared Drive by some users
  •   •  Cultural change resistance and reliance on old systems
  •   •  Confusion about how to approach file management & file migration from old systems

Key Recommendations

A User-Centered Redesign


This user-centered redesign can be broken up into three categories: Structural/Design Recomendations; Implementational Recomendations and Organizational Recomendations.


Design Recomendations.


  •   •  Use of a single Google Drive Structure.
Google drive can be set up for an entire organization to operate with one structure. This will concretize the structure and consistency of folder names and locations; avoid confusion over multiple individuals filing systems; allow for administration from a single point or individual and inherently standardize structure and look.


  •   •  Streamlined File Naming Convention
Simplify the file naming convention: reduce from 5 components to 2 components. Simpler is better even if it conveys less information due to the fact that it will be used period. Complexity leads to no use, inconsistency to no value.


Implementational Recomendations.


  •   •  Engage Users in Redesign Process.
Engage all employees throughout the design and implementation processes. Do user research and needs assessment and show this process as a benefit, not a burden. When people feel their needs have been considered and they feel that they are part of a change effort that change is much more likely to be permanent.


  •   •  Change Implementation Strategy.
  •   ·  Unfreeze: Communicate change process, necessity of change
  •   ·  Change: Implement change, engage users, abate change resistence and garner feedback.
  •   ·  Refreeze: Reward change champions and monitor outcomes.


Kurt Lewin’s 3 Stage Change Model

Organizational Recomendations.


  •   •  System Administrator(s).
We recognize that it may not be possible to hire on someone for this job. However we do believe that these responsbilities assigned to a single or more would significantly assist with non-adherence: Manage Permissions, Monitor Adherence, Design the file structure, Continuous Improvement.


  •   •  Training and Superusers.
Post design we suggest the following training elements:
  •   ·  Provide initial training session to educate team on Google Drive best practices, permissions, functionality.
  •   ·  Secondary training on organizational Google Drive structure.
  •   ·  Create or reinstate super-users at the team level.
  •   •  One Page Printed Reference Guide.
  •   ·  Include Administrator and Team Expert Contacts.
  •   ·  Visualization of single Drive Structure.
  •   ·  Quick Reference with example of File Naming Convention.


Conclusion

LSA SAA is a student centered organization dedicated to the development and growth of undergraduate students at University of Michigan. This function will always come first for the LSA SAA, therefore processes in place to support student interaction must use the least complex solutions to work well for all staff members. Existing file management procedures have faltered due to the complexity of protocols and the manner in which the last change iteration was rolled out. The existing design iteration is not supported by users in the office because they feel their needs have heard; as a result, staff feel estranged by the change process as opposed to an integral part of it. To create a new file management system that addresses these problems we recommend LSA SAA focus on user research in the next design iteration and implement our streamlined design recommendations centered on reducing complexity and redundancies within file management protocols. By utilizing these suggestions LSA SAA can better integrate file management into the workflow of users, garner ground-up support for change, and create processes that connect the potential value of the system to everyday use.

Bike Down Memory Lane

In this project, our team utilized Visual Reality to provide realistic expeience of cycling indoors. Audio, vison and sense of touch came one by one. Fisrt, people can only hear the sound and when they start pedaling they can see, hear and feel the wind and environment at the sam time. Tp achieve these, we used Arduino, TouchDesigner, Oculus, Accelerometer and bike with trainer.
Skill
Visual Reality Video Making
Arduino Coding
Tool
Adobe Premiere.
iMovie.
Arduino.
Media Metadata Injector.

Motivation

People nowadays spend too much time indoors, in front of computers, working and studying, so our design motivation was to let people experience indoor cycling and motivate them to go outside for some fresh air and exercises.

Design Process

We first brainstormed how we can recall people’s memory and what senses do we need to make a memory realistic. According to Condillac’s Statue, we wanted to start with sense of smell and finally sense of touch with an installation let people experience each senses in sequence. But then we figured out we wanted something more realistic, so we decided to use Visual Reality.
However, we wanted to engage participants in different senses one by one, which cannot be achieved only by VR. So we came up with an idea using bicycle and show them cycling scenes.
After finalizing the theme of our project, we started building the storyline and breaking down the project into VR, audio and video aspects.

Timeline.


So our storyline was: first, participants put on headset and earphones when they can only see darkness but hear people talking and sounds in the environment; and then they will be led to the bicycle and get on it; When they start pedaling, they will see the downhill scenes and people cycling beside them; at the same time, we will use a table fan to provide them the feeling of wind and use accelerometer to send their movement to Arduino providing real time speed, which makes the speed more realistic and refreshing.


Sketche of Timeline.

Components.


I chose 3 logotypes from my sketches and created them in Adobe Illustrator. I used Segoe Script for those logos, becasue the script is stylish. Since the customer wanted something simple but fasionable, I chose a fancy typeface to accompany the simple logo, each logo involved a simple design. If both were simple, it would be dull.

My Role

VR Vedio Maker.


My role in this project was focusing on video making.
First, I searched 360 degree videos in high quality for VR online and found the Trike Drifting video in 360 degrees that we used later on. And then I edited this video in Adobe Premiere, adding darkness prior to the video for only audio echoing without vision, cutting down useless parts, extending the length and making the whole video smooth. After that, I combined the audio into the video and used Spatial Media Metadata Injector to make this video 360 degrees for VR use.

Arduino Coder.


In the process of bringing those parts together, I also helped with the coding in Arduino. The video playing speed was so sensitive to the original speed of the pedaling, so every real time speed was sent to the TouchDesigner, making the video unstable. So we decided to only use the peak values for one rotating of the pedal, making the speed less sensitive and stable. I helped writing the code in Arduino to extract only peak values as outputs to control the speed of video playing.

Famous Architects Alphabet Poster

In this project, I designed an English Alphabet Poster whose theme was Famouse Architects. I choosed the most widely-used names of architects, for example, Zaha- first name of Zaha Hadid and Khan- last name of Louis Khan. I picked black- the favorite color of architects as the basic color and script-look as the typeface. This poster is for everyone who is engaged or interested in Architecture including myself to know and remember those world famous architects.
Target Population
Architects.
Designers.
Architecture buffs.
Skill
Graphic Design.
Tool
Adobe Illustrator.
Adobe Photoshop.

Concept

Color Palette


Architects like simple color and always dark color, such as black and gray. As a fan of Architecture, I wanted to keep this style of architects but added some lively bright colors to avoid dullness. I first looked through many color palettes on Adobe Color CC and choosed this classical one below as my basic color pallete. And generated more colors based on Monochromatic Harmony.


Typeface: Segoe Print.


Architects are doing sketches all the time, so I wanted the font to be like their handwriting letters. Font Segoe Print is styled and is based on handwriting letters, and at the same time it is clear to read.


Famous Architects Alphabet Poster (Tabloid 11X17”).

Design Process

Sketches.


I sketched the overall layout and color arrangement of this poster. Beside, I also sketched out the designs for 4 models.


Alphabet Sketches of Layout and Models.


Color Palette.

Models.


I first created models for N, W, I, A, O, and modules that contain longest (V for Van de Rohe) and shortest words (R for Rem). The first step after I selected the images of those architecs was masking those pictures with color palette and thick letters in Photoshop.
